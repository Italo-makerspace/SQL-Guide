DCL: The Security Manager's Language

The DCL (Data Control Language) manages security and access permissions. Think of it as the library's security manager, who decides who is allowed to do what. It operates using three primary commands.

  • GRANT (Granting Access): To hand over the key, allowing a user to access a book or a shelf.
    Example: GRANT SELECT ON Books TO librarian;
  • DENY (Explicitly Forbidding): To prohibit a user from performing a specific action, even if they have general permissions.
    Example: DENY DELETE ON Books TO librarian;
  • REVOKE (Revoking Access): To take the key back, removing a permission that was previously granted.
    Example: REVOKE INSERT ON Books FROM librarian;

In summary, you GRANT to permit, DENY to forbid, and REVOKE to take away. DCL is essential for maintaining the security of any system.
